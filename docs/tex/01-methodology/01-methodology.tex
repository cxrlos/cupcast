\begin{abstract}
I specify a forecasting model for the 2026 FIFA World Cup: a Dixon--Coles goal model \cite{dixoncoles1997} fitted by maximum likelihood on a decade of international results with exponential time-decay weighting, whose team ratings are shrunk toward a prior built from minutes-weighted, performance-based squad-quality composites. Match probabilities feed a Monte Carlo simulation of the full 48-team tournament under FIFA's official group tiebreakers and Round-of-32 bracket allocation. This paper documents the data, the rating system, the model equations, the estimation procedure, and the simulation design. Validation is reported in Paper~II; the forecast itself in Paper~III.
\end{abstract}

\section{Elo Ratings}
The rating input follows the World Football Elo system \cite{eloratings}, whose
predictive value for match outcomes is established empirically by
\citet{hvattumarntzen2010}. After each match the rating of team $i$ updates as
\begin{equation}
  R_i' = R_i + K \, G \,(W - W_e), \qquad
  W_e = \frac{1}{1 + 10^{-(R_i - R_j + H)/400}},
\end{equation}
where $K$ encodes match importance, $G$ is the goal-difference multiplier, $W$ the
realised result, and $H$ the home adjustment. Computing ratings from raw history
(rather than scraping published tables) yields reproducible as-of-date ratings for
backtesting.

\section{The Goal Model}
Goals scored by teams $i$ and $j$ are modelled as conditionally Poisson with rates
\begin{equation}
  \lambda_i = \exp\!\bigl(\mu + \att_i - \dfn_j + \gamma\, h_i\bigr), \qquad
  \lambda_j = \exp\!\bigl(\mu + \att_j - \dfn_i\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $\att$ and $\dfn$ are attack and defense ratings, and the host indicator $h_i$
is nonzero only for Mexico, the USA, and Canada in their home venues --- the 2026
format makes every other fixture neutral. Independence is corrected at low scores by
the Dixon--Coles adjustment \cite{dixoncoles1997}
\begin{equation}
  \Prob(X=x, Y=y) = \tau_{\rho}(x,y)\,
    \frac{e^{-\lambda_i}\lambda_i^{x}}{x!}\,
    \frac{e^{-\lambda_j}\lambda_j^{y}}{y!},
\end{equation}
with $\tau_\rho$ reweighting the $\{0,1\}\times\{0,1\}$ scorelines; this addresses
the draw underestimation of the independent-Poisson model of \citet{maher1982}.
Training matches are weighted by recency,
\begin{equation}
  \phi(t) = \exp(-\xi\, \Delta t),
\end{equation}
with $\xi$ selected by out-of-sample predictive likelihood, following the evidence in
\citet{leyetal2019} that smooth decay outperforms fixed windows for national teams.
Friendlies receive an additional importance down-weight, mirroring the Elo $K$ hierarchy.

\section{Shrinkage Toward the Squad Prior}
Fitted ratings are blended with the squad-composite-implied prior,
\begin{equation}
  \tilde{\att}_i = w_i\, \hat{\att}_i + (1 - w_i)\, \att_i^{\mathrm{prior}}, \qquad
  w_i = \frac{n_i^{\mathrm{eff}}}{n_i^{\mathrm{eff}} + k},
\end{equation}
where $n_i^{\mathrm{eff}}$ is the decay-weighted match count, so teams with sparse
competitive history lean on squad quality while data-rich teams keep their fitted
ratings --- the partial-pooling logic of hierarchical models \cite{baioblangiardo2010}
in a transparent two-stage form.
